I’m not actually a wild animal

The reality is that llamas are a domesticated animal. While they can be found in the wild, they are no longer wild animals – they’re pack animals.

I can spit at your enemies 15 feet away

While llamas don’t always spit, it’s a clear sign that they are annoyed at something, or someone.

I am your guard Llama

Sheep and alpaca farmers in the US discovered that llamas were great guard animals, as long as you only have one per herd of sheep.

You don’t need your therapy fish when you have me!

In parts of the US you can find therapy llamas working in nursing homes and schools where they comfort the residents and patients.

Person-ality

Because we're people too :)

Social Butterfly

Llamas are very social animals, so it makes sense that they need a way to communicate other than sticking their tongues out in disgust or spitting. One of the ways they communicate is to hum to each other in a range of tones, all with seemingly different meanings.

Spits at people you don’t like

Llamas are known as animals that spit when provoked or threatened. They can spit at humans also, but they usually spit at each other to solve misunderstandings in the group.

Resemble the habbits of yourself

Llamas are very intelligent, but stubborn animals. When the load on its back is too heavy, llama will refuse to carry it until some of the load is removed.
